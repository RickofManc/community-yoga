Yoga in Class

When starting something new, or going to a new place, you may have some questions about how things work, what to wear or what to bring.

We’ve done our best to answer some of the most frequently asked questions.

If something isn’t listed below or you’d like some more assistance don’t hesitate to email Marie at marie@otyoga.com or by telephone on 07399487430.

I have never been to a class before. Where do I start?

Don’t worry, you are in safe hands – thousands of people take their first ever yoga class everyday around the world! Our guided yoga supports beginners through to experts.

Where is OT Yoga how do I get there?

Old Trafford Yoga is always located at the St John’s Centre, St John’s Road, Old Trafford, M16 7GX. Information on how to get to us can be planned using this Google Maps link.

Can I park at OT Yoga?

Whilst there is no on-site car parking, there is ample on-road parking surrounding the St John’s Centre.

What is the minimum age for attending a class?

The minimum age for attending all class is 16. Children aged 14 or 15 may attend all other classes with a guardian who is over 16 at the class Marie’s discretion. If you would like to bring your 14 or 15 year old child to a class please contact Marie.

Do I need to book classes before attending?

No, currently the hall is large enough to accommodate more than enough students, however this could change if the number of attendees grows significantly.

How much does a class cost?

Each class costs £5. Please pay this in cash to Marie at the end of each class – the correct amount will always be appreciated!

What do I wear?

Some people wear baggy, loose fitting bottoms and a t-shirt. Other people wear tight stretchy leggings and tops. The only thing that matters is that you wear comfortable clothes that you can easily move and stretch in. As you move through a class you can get warm so it’s good to wear clothes that keep you cool, but bring a jumper for relaxation, when the body rapidly cools down. Some people also bring socks for the relaxation phase.

What shall I bring?

If you have a mat, please bring it. If not, we can provide one for the duration of the class. Water for before and after the class would be advisable.

When should I arrive for my class?

If you are a new to OT Yoga, please arrive 15 minutes early for your first session to have an informal chat with Marie to get to know each other. If you are a regular student, please arrive 10 minutes before the class starts so you have plenty of time to get settled and clear your mind. We realise lateness is often unavoidable but to avoid disruption, we cannot allow latecomers into class 10 minutes after it has started.

Can I go to a class if I am pregnant?

Yoga is excellent during pregnancy and for you and baby after the birth. However it is recommended that you do not practice yoga in your first trimester. You can return to practice in your second and third trimesters, where yoga is a fantastic support.

Is there an etiquette guide?

Yes! 10 rules to know and follow that will allow everyone to enjoy OT Yoga;

  1. Take your shoes off when you enter the hall.
  2. Be mindful of where you place yourself in the studio (and don’t be greedy).
  3. Respect a fellow students space – avoid stepping on others’ mats.
  4. Breathe, but not too loudly.
  5. Don’t check your phone.
